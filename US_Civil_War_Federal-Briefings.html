Federal Briefing: January 29, 2029
Last updated: January 29, 2029

Current Situation

The situation across the United States continues to be highly volatile, with several regions in active conflict. As of the latest reports, National Guard units have been deployed in key areas to maintain order. The curfew has been extended to 24 hours in most high-risk zones, with military operations underway to suppress hostile groups.

Key Updates:

  • Full mobilization continues under Executive Directive 5105-A.
  • New curfew in effect across multiple states. 24-hour lockdown initiated in select regions.
  • FEMA operations in full force, providing critical relief in most impacted areas.

Public Instructions:

  • Stay indoors during the curfew (8 PM to 6 AM local time).
  • Call 1-800-FED-SAFE for emergency assistance and reporting incidents.
  • Sign up for regional alerts via Ready.gov to receive updated instructions.
Federal Briefing: January 28, 2029
Last updated: January 28, 2029

Current Situation

Following the escalation of armed insurgencies across the nation, federal authorities have begun consolidating forces in strategic locations. The American Freedom Nation (AFN) has claimed control over several urban centers, while the Department of Homeland Security continues to assess the situation.

Key Updates:

  • President Veronica Aldridge has called for an emergency session of Congress to discuss further actions.
  • Increased intelligence operations underway to locate and disrupt insurgent cells.
  • Temporary suspension of postal services in hostile zones to prevent insurgent movement.

Public Instructions:

  • Remain vigilant and avoid traveling to high-risk areas.
  • Secure all windows and doors during curfew hours. Follow local directives from federal agents.
